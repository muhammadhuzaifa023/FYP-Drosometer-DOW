Eye Colour



The color of the eye is determined by the presence of different pigments produced by specialized cells called ommochrome cells. These pigments are synthesized from amino acids through a series of enzymatic reactions. The production of pigments is regulated by a group of genes called the pigmentation pathway genes. Mutations in these genes can result in changes in eye color or even complete loss of pigmentation. Eye color in Drosophila can also be influenced by environmental factors such as temperature, diet, and exposure to certain chemicals or radiation. In addition, eye color can change during the fly's lifetime as a result of aging or injury. The changes in eye color are often associated with changes in pigmentation gene expression.
