Eye Ommatidium



The Drosophila eye is a compound eye, which is made up of many identical subunits called ommatidia. Each ommatidium is a functional unit that contains a lens, pigment cells, and eight photoreceptor cells. The photoreceptor cells are arranged in a specific pattern and are responsible for detecting light and transmitting visual signals to the brain. The arrangement of ommatidia in the Drosophila eye is highly organized and follows a repeating hexagonal pattern. This organization is thought to help the fly detect and track movement in its environment more efficiently. The number of ommatidia in the Drosophila eye varies between different species of the fly, but in general, the number is in the range of 500 to 800.
