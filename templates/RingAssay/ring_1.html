Ring Assay



The Drosophila Ring Assay is a significant technique used to assess climbing behavior and geotaxis response in fruit flies. It involves placing the flies in vertical test tubes lined with agar and measuring their climbing distance, which provides insights into their motor function and coordination. This assay is instrumental in genetic studies, disease modeling, and drug screening, allowing researchers to identify gene mutations, understand neurobiological mechanisms, and discover potential therapies for neurological disorders. Its simplicity and reproducibility make it an indispensable tool for gaining comprehensive insights into Drosophila behavior and its implications in various fields of research.



Drosophila Ring Assay module measures the distance of flies from the bottom to the top of a test tube. By uploading an image, the application automatically detects and circles each fly, assigning a unique number and displaying their respective height values in a convenient table.
