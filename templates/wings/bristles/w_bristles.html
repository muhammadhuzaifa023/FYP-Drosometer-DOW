Wing Bristles



Bristles on the Drosophila wing are not just decorative structures; they serve several important functions in the fly's life cycle. Bristles on the wing margin help the fly to maintain its position while perched, while bristles on the wing surface provide sensory information that is critical for the fly's ability to navigate and avoid obstacles during flight. The number and distribution of bristles on the wing are controlled by a complex interplay of genetic and environmental factors, making the Drosophila wing an ideal model system for studying the development and evolution of these structures.



The Bristle Counting module is an essential component of our web application, designed to assist researchers in analyzing the bristle count within a custom region of the Drosophila wing. Using this module, researchers can select a region of interest within an uploaded image of the Drosophila wing and obtain an accurate count of the number of bristles present within that region.
